## Answers

**1.** B) Static friction cancels your applied force
   *Explanation: Correct! High friction = applied force, so net force = 0*

   *Explanation: Correct! Equal and opposite momentums cancel, resulting in zero*

   *Explanation: Correct! Friction equals applied force for constant velocity (net force = 0)*

   *Explanation: Correct! Wall exerts equal and opposite force on you (Newton's 3rd law)*

   *Explanation: Correct! Truck has greater mass, so greater momentum (p = mv)*

   *Explanation: Correct! Total momentum before = total momentum after*

**7.** B) Total momentum remains constant in absence of external force
   *Explanation: Correct! Total momentum before = total momentum after (isolated system)*

**8.** B) kg·m/s
   *Explanation: Correct! Momentum = mass (kg) × velocity (m/s)*

**9.** B) For every action, there's equal and opposite reaction
   *Explanation: Correct! Action and reaction forces are equal and opposite*

**10.** B) F = ma
   *Explanation: Correct! Force equals mass times acceleration*

**11.** B) An unbalanced external force acts on it
   *Explanation: Correct! External unbalanced force is needed to change motion state*

**12.** B) Mass × velocity
   *Explanation: Correct! Momentum (p) = mass (m) × velocity (v)*

**13.** B) Force of friction opposes the motion
   *Explanation: Correct! Friction between ball and ground slows it down*

**14.** B) Inertia can make it fall during sudden motion changes
   *Explanation: Correct! Sudden acceleration/braking causes luggage to move due to inertia*

**15.** B) Inertia of dust resists carpet's motion
   *Explanation: Correct! Dust stays at rest while carpet moves, causing separation*

**16.** B) Reaction force pushes hose backward
   *Explanation: Correct! Water going forward creates backward reaction force*

**17.** B) Stone
   *Explanation: Correct! Stone has greater mass, therefore more inertia*

**18.** B) Train
   *Explanation: Correct! Train has massive weight, therefore huge inertia*

**19.** B) Mass
   *Explanation: Correct! Inertia is directly proportional to mass*

**20.** B) Inertia of leaves resists branch motion
   *Explanation: Correct! Leaves tend to stay at rest due to inertia while branch moves*

**21.** B) Inertia keeps your body moving forward
   *Explanation: Correct! Your body resists the change from motion to rest*

**22.** B) Inertia keeps your body at rest while bus moves
   *Explanation: Correct! Your body resists the change from rest to motion*

**23.** 3rd OR third OR Third

**24.** B) Force is rate of change of momentum
   *Explanation: Correct! F = Δp/Δt (change in momentum per unit time)*

**25.** B) Ground pushes horse forward in reaction to horse pushing ground back
   *Explanation: Correct! Horse-ground interaction moves the system forward*

   *Explanation: During melting, all the supplied heat energy is used to break the intermolecular forces in ice (latent heat of fusion), so temperature stays constant until all ice melts.*

   *Explanation: Once all ice has melted, the heat supplied is used to increase the kinetic energy of water molecules, causing the temperature to rise.*

**7.** A) Cooling effect
   *Explanation: Evaporation causes cooling because the liquid particles that evaporate take away heat energy (latent heat of vaporization) from the surroundings.*

**8.** A) Gaseous state
   *Explanation: In the gaseous state, particles move randomly with high kinetic energy and have no fixed positions, resulting in no orderly arrangement.*

**9.** A) Pascal
   *Explanation: Pascal (Pa) is the SI unit of pressure, defined as one newton per square meter (N/m²).*

**10.** A) Density
   *Explanation: Density is defined as mass per unit volume, so its SI unit is kilogram per cubic metre (kg/m³).*

**11.** A) Osmosis involves movement of solvent through a semi-permeable membrane, while diffusion does not require a membrane
   *Explanation: Osmosis is a special type of diffusion where only the solvent (usually water) moves through a semi-permeable membrane from higher to lower concentration.*

**12.** A) Higher concentration to lower concentration
   *Explanation: Both osmosis and diffusion follow the concentration gradient, with particles moving from regions of higher concentration to regions of lower concentration.*

**13.** A) Osmosis
   *Explanation: Water moves into the raisin through its semi-permeable skin from higher water concentration (outside) to lower water concentration (inside), which is osmosis.*

   *Explanation: The aroma particles move from higher concentration (near the oven) to lower concentration (rest of the house) through the air, which is diffusion.*

   *Explanation: Steam at 100°C has absorbed extra energy (latent heat of vaporization) to change from liquid to gas. When it condenses on skin, it releases this extra energy, causing severe burns.*

**17.** A) 0°C
   *Explanation: Water freezes and turns into ice at 0°C (or 273 K) at standard atmospheric pressure.*

   *Explanation: During boiling, all the heat energy supplied is used to convert water into steam (latent heat of vaporization), so the temperature remains constant at the boiling point.*

**20.** A) 0°C
   *Explanation: The temperature falls to 0°C and remains constant while water converts to ice. During this phase change, latent heat of fusion is released, so temperature stays at 0°C until all water freezes.*

**21.** A) It continues to decrease below 0°C
   *Explanation: Once all water has frozen into ice, the temperature of ice can decrease further below 0°C as it continues to lose heat to the freezer environment.*

   *Explanation: A sponge has the characteristic properties of a solid (definite shape and volume). It compresses because of air-filled pores, not because it lacks solid structure.*

   *Explanation: During melting, all supplied heat is used to increase the distance between molecules (overcoming intermolecular forces) rather than increasing their kinetic energy, so temperature stays constant.*

   *Explanation: During melting, molecules move farther apart as intermolecular forces are partially overcome, increasing the spacing between particles while temperature remains constant.*
